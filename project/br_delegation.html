The Beginning of .br (1989–1991)

The origins of the Internet in Brazil cannot be understood without looking at its international background. In the United States, the ARPANET had already proven the use of packet switching as a reliable way of connecting computers across distances. These early experiments became the reference model for many nations, including Brazil, that aimed to build their own academic networks.

First IP Blocks (1990)

In the 1980s, universities and research centers started to experiment with data communication. Among the pioneers were the USP, Unicamp, LNCC, and the UFRJ. These institutions connected to international networks such as BITNET, allowing the exchange of academic messages and collaboration with foreign researchers.

First Connections and Structure (1991)

A key milestone came in 1987, when the DARPA and the NSF authorized the UFRJ to officially connect to the Internet in the United States. Soon after, in 1989, the Brazilian government launched the RNP, the first nationwide academic backbone, which interconnected universities and research institutions. This initiative created a collaborative scientific environment and laid the foundation for the future TCP/IP expansion across the country.
